LatticeModel

Lattice-gap prediction in particle physics, with error bars

Mendeleev predicted gallium from a gap in a table. Gell-Mann predicted the Ω⁻ the same way. This project asks — and measures — how much better than chance that inference pattern actually is, then registers its own falsifiable predictions.

Historical precision audit

Sixteen sourced gap-predictions from η (1961) to superpartners: precision 67–89% across two blind raters; every failure was a beyond-pattern extrapolation.

Null-model baselines

Leave-one-out recovery vs chance, permutation tests, jitter robustness, and the operating curve behind the headline numbers.
The headline numbers.
  • 46 leave-one-out ablations: true cells rank at mean percentile 0.137 vs 0.5 under chance (p ≈ 7×10⁻¹⁸).
  • Searching the top 5% of candidates by proximity captures 63% of true cells — 12.6× the chance rate.
  • Ω⁻ mass reproduced to 0.8% (GMO), τ mass to 0.008% (Koide) — both as passing unit tests.
  • Held-out validation: Ω_cc⁺ predicted at 3 775 MeV; LHCb measured ~3 727 (June 2026).
