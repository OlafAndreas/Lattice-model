assigned by a single rater from the prediction bases; the design's
blind two-rater protocol still requires a second independent pass.

## Precision

- closed predictions: **12** (8 confirmed, 4 refuted); 4 open (excluded)
- **precision = 66.7%**, Wilson 95% interval [39.1%, 86.2%]

### Sensitivity to contested grouping choices

- baseline: one entry per prediction program: 66.7% [39.1%, 86.2%]
- 4th generation counted as four species: 53.3% [30.1%, 75.2%]
- antidecuplet counted as one program: 72.7% [43.4%, 90.3%]
- free quarks excluded (hedged original prediction): 72.7% [43.4%, 90.3%]

## Inter-rater reliability (blind second-rater pass, 2026-07-17)

A second independent rater received only the redacted prediction
texts and the pre-registered criteria — no outcomes, statuses, or
first-rater labels. (Caveat: like any expert rater, not blind to
world knowledge of famous outcomes.)

- **within/beyond labels: 100% agreement, Cohen's κ = 1.00** — the load-bearing classification is fully corroborated
- in-class labels: 56% agreement; the second rater is stricter, excluding dynamics-derived multiplets and mechanism-based predictions: Theta+(1540), Xi3/2--(1862), free fractional-charge quarks, glueballs, magnetic monopole, axion, superpartners (naturalness window)
- strict (rater-2) precision: **8/9 = 88.9%** [56.5%, 98.0%] — precision *rises* under the stricter reading
- within/beyond Fisher probability: 0.002 (inclusive labels) vs 0.11 (strict labels — only one beyond-pattern prediction survives as in-class, so the split stays directionally identical but loses small-n significance)

Reconciliation: both readings are defensible under the pre-registered wording (criterion 1 is ambiguous for multiplets *derived from* dynamics); we therefore report the rater range 67–89% rather than forcing one value.

## Within- vs beyond-pattern split

- within-pattern completions: **8/8 confirmed**
- beyond-pattern extrapolations: **0/4 confirmed**
- Fisher exact probability of this split under a random null: **p ≈ 0.002**

Failures concentrate exactly where the ablation experiments'
isolated-state finding predicts: positing a new copy of a pattern
(a fourth generation, a new antidecuplet, a new state category)
fails; completing a partially-filled pattern succeeds.
